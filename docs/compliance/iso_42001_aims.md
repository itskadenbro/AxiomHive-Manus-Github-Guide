# ISO/IEC 42001:2023 AI Management System (AIMS)

AxiomHive is designed to align with the **ISO/IEC 42001:2023** standard, providing a structured framework for the governance of artificial intelligence systems.

## 1. Leadership and Organizational Context

AxiomHive's governance structure is overseen by the **Ethical Oversight Committee (EOC)**, which ensures that all AI development and deployment activities align with the organization's strategic objectives and the **Non-Harm Principle**.

## 2. AI Policy and Objectives

The AxiomHive AI Policy is founded on the following core objectives:
- **Trustworthiness**: Ensuring AI systems are valid, reliable, and safe.
- **Transparency**: Providing clear insights into AI decision-making processes.
- **Accountability**: Maintaining human oversight for all high-stakes decisions.
- **Continuous Improvement**: Regularly refining AI governance based on performance and regulatory updates.

## 3. Risk Management and Impact Assessment

AxiomHive implements a rigorous risk management process:
- **Risk Identification**: Systematically identifying technical, ethical, and legal risks.
- **Impact Assessment**: Evaluating the potential societal and environmental impacts of AI systems.
- **Control Implementation**: Applying robust technical and administrative controls to mitigate identified risks.

## 4. Operational Model: Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA)

| Phase | Activity |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Plan** | Define the scope of the AIMS, identify controls, and evaluate risks. |
| **Do** | Implement AI governance policies and ethical practices. |
| **Check** | Monitor AI performance and ensure compliance with global regulations. |
| **Act** | Refine AI governance based on performance outcomes and technological progress. |

## 5. System Lifecycle Management

AxiomHive manages the entire AI lifecycle, from data collection and model building to validation and secure deployment, ensuring integrity at every stage.
